## Official Perspective: More Than Just an Investment

According to the latest official guidelines from the HKSAR Government (InvestHK and the Immigration Department), the New Capital Investment Entrant Scheme (New CIES) operates under a rigorous compliance framework. While many believe having HK$30 million is the only requirement, the official rules specify strict criteria for **asset source, holding methods, and investment timelines**.

This guide synthesizes official requirements with frontline practical experience to help you navigate each critical stage.

---

## 1. Eligibility Criteria: Who Can Apply?

The official guidelines state that applicants must meet the following basic conditions:
- **Age**: 18 years or older.
- **Scope**: 
  - Foreign nationals (excluding Afghanistan, Cuba, and North Korea).
  - Chinese nationals with foreign permanent residency (Mainland residents must obtain a third-country PR first).
  - Macao SAR residents and Taiwan Chinese residents.
- **Net Assets**: Absolute beneficial ownership of net assets with a market value of at least **HK$30 million** throughout the 6 months before applying for net asset assessment.
- **Clean Record**: No adverse record and compliance with general immigration and security requirements.
- **Self-Sufficiency**: Ability to demonstrate financial support and accommodation for self and dependants.

---

## 2. Application Procedure: The Seven Official Steps

The official process is standardized into seven stages:

1.  **Net Asset Assessment**: Submit an application to the New CIES Office (InvestHK) with a Fulfillment document issued by a Certified Public Accountant (CPA).
2.  **Approval-in-Principle**: The Immigration Department issues an "Approval-in-Principle," allowing the applicant to stay in HK as a visitor for up to **180 days** to make the investment.
3.  **Investment Compliance Assessment**: After making the investment within the specified timeframe, submit proof to InvestHK.
4.  **Formal Approval**: Formal approval from the Immigration Department, granting a stay of not less than **24 months**.
5.  **Ongoing Compliance**: On each anniversary of the formal approval, submit proof of asset maintenance to InvestHK.
6.  **Extension of Stay**: Apply for extensions (typically in a 3+3 year pattern) after the initial 2 years.
7.  **Permanent Residency**: After at least **7 years** of continuous ordinary residence in Hong Kong, apply for HK Permanent Identity Card.

---

## 3. Permissible Investment Assets: The HK$30M Breakdown

Official rules divide the HK$30 million investment into two components:

### A. Permissible Investment Assets (HK$27 Million)
These assets must be held by an eligible financial intermediary (bank, broker, or insurer):
- **Equities**: Securities listed on the HKEX.
- **Debt Securities**: Listed bonds, government or agency debts denominated in HKD or RMB.
- **Certificates of Deposit (CDs)**: Issued by authorized institutions, **capped at HK$3 million**, with at least 12 months to maturity at the time of purchase.
- **Eligible Collective Investment Schemes**: Includes SFC-authorized funds, REITs, **Investment-Linked Assurance Schemes (ILI)**, and Open-ended Fund Companies (OFCs).
- **Limited Partnership Funds (LPFs)**: Ownership interest, with a contribution cap of **HK$10 million**.
- **Real Estate**:
  - **Non-residential**: Commercial or industrial properties, capped at **HK$10 million**.
  - **Residential**: Transaction value must be **HK$30 million or above**, but only **HK$10 million** can be counted towards the CIES investment.

### B. CIES Portfolio (HK$3 Million)
A fixed **HK$3 million** must be invested in the CIES Portfolio established and managed by the Hong Kong Investment Corporation Limited (HKIC) to support local industry development. This portion has a lock-up period.

---

## 4. Holding Methods and Timeline

The official guidelines offer flexibility:
- **Individual Name**: Accounts opened directly in the applicant's name.
- **Holding Company**: Held via a company 100% owned by the applicant (registered in HK or other eligible jurisdictions).
- **Timeline**: 
  - Investments made within 180 days before the net asset assessment application.
  - Investments completed within 180 days after receiving "Approval-in-Principle."

## 5. Real Costs and Audit Pitfalls

The official process strictly requires reports **issued by Certified Public Accountants (CPA)**.

| Item | Est. Cost | Description |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Advisory Fee | **HK$80k–100k** | Full coordination with official bodies |
| CPA Audit Report | **~HK$20k per report** | Critical doc; cost varies by asset complexity |
| Translation/Notary | HK$5k–20k | For Mainland and overseas documents |

> 💡 **Expert Tip**: While the initial net asset assessment requires verifying your existing assets over the past 6 months, the real advantage of **ILI (Investment-Linked Assurance Schemes)** begins **after the investment is made**. By using ILI as your investment vehicle, all underlying fund switches and reporting are handled by the insurer. For subsequent annual compliance reports, you typically only need one statement provided by the insurance company, significantly reducing audit complexity and long-term administrative costs.

## 6. Document Checklist

1.  **Personal**: Recent photo, travel document (passport), third-country PR proof (mandatory for Mainland residents).
2.  **Asset Proof**: CPA-issued asset statement, fulfillment documents, bank/broker statements (last 6 months).
3.  **Investment Proof**: Contract with financial intermediary, trade confirmations/statements, property deeds (if applicable).
4.  **Family**: Marriage certificate, birth certificates for children.
